About the App

Using cutting-edge virtual reality technology, the TrinityVR mobile app offers users the chance to immerse themselves in the sights, sounds, and stories of Trinity College Dublin. Presented through beautiful 360° photos, videos, and spatialised audio, users are able to navigate and explore the campus at will—including some building interiors that can’t even be visited in person.

Designed with potential visitors to Ireland in mind, the app highlights the school’s rich history, architecture, and community. This content is conveyed through a variety of interactive multimedia, including images, videos, animated 3D renderings, and personal interviews.

The app is available on both iOS and Android mobile phones and is best experienced with a virtual reality headset and headphones.

Commonly Asked Questions

You mention the murder of Edward Ford in Trinity’s Rubrics building. Could you tell me some more about what happened that night?

The unabridged story goes that on March 7, 1734, a group of students entered Front Gate at night. On campus, they beat up a porter, which earned them a sound scolding from Ford. The students, already at odds with Ford, decided to break the windows in his room. As you know, the altercation escalated and Ford was shot. Before he died, he asked that the students be forgiven. The four accused students were later acquitted of the crime by the court, but not the school board. They were later expelled. But the story doesn’t end there. Some say you can see Ford’s ghost wandering the campus to this day.

How did you manage to take these beautiful images of Trinity without anyone appearing in them?

Our photographer Adrian got up really, REALLY early. On a Saturday, no less! As for the weather, we just got lucky.

What do the star icons on some of the markers represent?

The points of interest with stars indicate the presence of 3D-rendered content. These images and animations help to illustrate Trinity’s rich past and bring it to life.

Exploring TrinityVR has made me want to visit Ireland more than ever. How can I find out more about events happening in Dublin and the best time to visit?

Check out http://www.visitdublin.comto see what’s on in the city!
